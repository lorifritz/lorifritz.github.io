The Seminole Tribe of Florida

There are many exciting things to see and learn about the Seminole Indians -- the original Florida natives. You will definitely want to come back for more! For an exciting twist to your vacation, spend some time in Seminole Country at this beautiful and traditional village attraction on the Hollywood Seminole Indian Reservation at the Seminole Hard Rock Hotel & Casino. Be sure to visit the Tribe’s Ah-Tah-Thi-Ki Museum and Billie Swamp Safari.

Latin life in Miami

Miami is synonymous with Latin flavor, from strong and sweet mojitos to sizzling salsa moves. While Cuba is a dominant culture, Miami is composed of a melting pot of people from across Latin America and the Caribbean. This diverse population brings with it an electric nightlife scene. From dance clubs to music halls, cocktail lounges and buzzy restaurants, visit a few hot spots to get a taste of Miami’s Latin flavor on a night out on the town.

Florida's Cowboys

Mention the word "cowboy" and visions of the American West come to mind. But long before Florida cowboys were pushing cattle along the Chisholm Trail, rough-hewn Crackers were carving out cattle country on Florida ranges. Cattlemen are still riding the Florida range, pushing over a million head of cattle. Arcadia’s Rodeo events include bull riding, bareback bronco riding, saddle bronco riding, steer wrestling, tie-down roping and barrel racing.

The Oldest City

St. Augustine boasts a year-round mild climate perfect for strolling “the oldest city’s” delightful historic district, with its cobblestone streets, quaint cafes, bars, unique shops, and bed and breakfast inns. Experience the beauty of an early morning that slowly comes alive with locals and tourists setting out on foot to explore significant landmarks including the Castillo de San Marcos, Lightner Museum, Flagler College, and Fort Matanzas.
