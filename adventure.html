Devil's Den

Bought in 1993 by our current owners, this natural wonder has been transformed from a simple scuba diving site to a world class attraction. Visit one of North America's most prehistoric places, Devil's Den Spring! This underground spring inside a dry cave is home to crystal clear water, year round 72 degree water temperatures, ancient rock formations with stalactites, and fossil beds dating back 33 million years that guarantee great memories..

Myakka River State Park

One of the oldest and largest state parks, Myakka protects one of the state's most diverse natural areas. The Myakka River flows through 58 square miles of wetlands, prairies, hammocks, and pinelands. Visitors can enjoy wildlife viewing from a boardwalk that stretches out over the Upper Myakka Lake and stroll along the canopy walkway. John Pennekamp Coral Reef

John Pennekamp Coral Reef State Park encompasses about 70 nautical square miles. While the beautiful mangrove swamps and tropical hammocks offer visitors unique experiences, it is the coral reefs and their marine life that bring most visitors to the park. Visitors can enjoy the view of the reef from a glass-bottom boat tour, or by scuba diving and/or snorkeling. Canoeing, kayaking, and fishing through the park's waters are popular activities.

Discovery Cove

Discovery Cove is an all-inclusive day resort where you and your family will experience exciting animal encounters in a breathtaking tropical atmosphere. Swim with bottlenose dolphins, sharks, and rays, feed tropical birds, snorkel through a saltwater reef, and play inches from sea otters all in one day. Discovery Cove is a paradise surrounded by lush landscaping, tropical reefs, winding rivers, a resort-style pool complete with waterfalls.
